## ğŸ“– The 16 Vedic Sutras {#sutras}

1. **Ekadhikena Purvena** - By one more than the previous one
2. **Nikhilam Navatashcaramam Dashatah** - All from 9 and last from 10

4. **Paraavartya Yojayet** - Transpose and Apply
5. **Shunyam Saamyasamuccaye** - When the sum is the same, that sum is zero
6. **Anurupye Shunyamanyat** - If one is in ratio, the other is zero
7. **Sankalana-vyavakalanabhyam** - By addition and by subtraction
8. **Puranapuranabhyam** - By the completion or non-completion
9. **Chalana-Kalanabhyam** - Differences and Similarities
10. **Yaavadunam** - Whatever the extent of its deficiency
11. **Vyashtisamanstih** - Part and Whole
12. **Shesanyankena Charamena** - The remainders by the last digit
13. **Sopaantyadvayamantyam** - The ultimate and twice the penultimate
14. **Ekanyunena Purvena** - By one less than the previous one
15. **Gunitasamuchyah** - The product of the sum is equal to the sum of the product
16. **Gunakasamuchyah** - The factors of the sum is equal to the sum of the factors

## ğŸ† Tips for Parents and Teachers

### Making Math Fun:

1. **Use Real Objects**: Candies, toys, coins for counting
2. **Make it a Game**: Competition makes practice fun
3. **Celebrate Mistakes**: They're learning opportunities
4. **Mix Methods**: Different problems, different approaches
5. **Daily Practice**: 10-15 minutes is enough
6. **Connect to Life**: Shopping, cooking, sports
7. **Use Technology**: Apps, videos, interactive games
8. **Tell Stories**: Math in context is memorable
9. **Be Patient**: Everyone learns at their own pace
10. **Praise Effort**: Not just correct answers

### Red Flags (When to Try Different Methods):

- Child is frustrated or anxious
- Constantly says "I hate math"
- Avoids math homework
- Can't remember facts despite practice
- Gets right answer but can't explain how

**Solution**: Try visual methods, hands-on activities, or break into smaller steps!
